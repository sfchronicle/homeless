6,686

A civic disgrace

The reduction of homelessness to the extent humanly possible must be San Francisco’s No. 1 priority.

EDITORIAL

July 3, 2016

* Each figure represents 2
{% include '_text/opinion.html' %}

On any given day there are an estimated 6,686 homeless adults in San Francisco, according to a point-in-time count conducted by volunteers at the end of January 2015. While that figure is the most widely circulated estimate on the number of people living without shelter in the city, other databases show as many as 10,000 people experienced homelessness over the course the 2015-16 fiscal year. The true figure is most likely even higher. See past Chronicle coverage for more on the how many people are homeless, and the costs associated with the population.
